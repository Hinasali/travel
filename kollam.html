KOLLAM

Kollam is a beautiful district located in the state of Kerala, India, known for its picturesque landscapes, beaches, lakes, and historical landmarks. It is a major hub for the country's coir industry and is sometimes called the "Coir Capital of the World." Kollam is situated on the Malabar Coast,along the Arabian Sea, and offers a mix of natural beauty, rich culture, and historical significance.

A quiet and less crowded beach compared to others in Kerala, Kollam Beach is great for a relaxing time. You can enjoy the beautiful views, take a walk along the shore, or visit the nearby Thirumullavaram Beach

jatayu earths center

Located at Chadayamangalam, this park is famous for the Jatayu statue, which is one of the largest in the world. The park is a blend of nature, art, and mythology. According to Hindu legend, Jatayu, a mythical eagle, attempted to rescue Sita from Ravana. The park features a cable car ride, rock climbing, and an adventure park, making it a perfect destination for both nature lovers and adventure seekers.

pularuvi water falls

Palaruvi Waterfalls is one of the most famous and beautiful waterfalls in Kerala, located in the Kollam district. The name "Palaruvi" means "Stream of Milk" in Malayalam, and it is aptly named due to the milky white appearance of the water as it cascades down the rocks. The waterfall is situated about 35 kilometers from the city of Kollam, in a lush, verdant environment near the Western Ghats.
