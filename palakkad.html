PALAKKAD

Palakkad is a beautiful district in the state of Kerala, India, known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and historical significance. It is often referred to as the "Gateway of Kerala" because of its location connecting Kerala to the rest of India through the Western Ghats

pallakkad fort

Located in the heart of the city, Palakkad Fort is a historic fort built in the 18th century by the Mysorean king Hyder Ali. The fort is surrounded by a moat and is a well-preserved monument with a park inside. It's a great place to learn about the history of the region and enjoy a relaxing stroll.

silent valley national park

This is one of the most pristine and biodiverse rainforests in India. Situated in the Western Ghats, Silent Valley is home to a variety of wildlife, including the endangered Lion-tailed Macaque. The park is perfect for trekkers, nature lovers, and anyone seeking a tranquil getaway.

attapadi

Attappady is a tribal area in Palakkad known for its natural beauty, waterfalls, and vibrant culture. It is also famous for its rich biodiversity, and visitors can trek through the dense forests or explore the tribal villages.

Malampuzha Dam and Garden

Located about 10 km from Palakkad town, the Malampuzha Dam is one of the major attractions in the region. It is set amidst beautiful hills and gardens, making it a perfect spot for picnics. You can also enjoy boating in the reservoir and visit the nearby snake park
