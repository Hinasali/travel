KASARKODE

Kasaragod is a coastal town in the northernmost part of Kerala, India. It is known for its beautiful beaches, historical significance, and cultural diversity. Kasaragod lies near the border with Karnataka and is a gateway to both Kerala and Karnataka. The town has a unique blend of Malayalam and Tulu-speaking communities, and it boasts a rich heritage of temples, forts, and cultural festivals.

ananthapuram lake temple

Located about 15 kilometers from Kasargod town, Ananthapura Lake Temple is the only lake temple in Kerala. The temple is dedicated to Lord Ananthapadmanabha and is located in the middle of a serene lake. The temple and its surrounding beauty offer a tranquil atmosphere.

parappa wild life sanctuary

Parappa Wildlife Sanctuary is a serene and picturesque wildlife sanctuary located in the Kasargod district of Kerala, India. Spanning across the lush greenery of the Western Ghats, the sanctuary covers an area of around 1,200 hectares and is known for its rich biodiversity.

nileshwar

Nileshwaram is known for its cultural richness and history. It's a place where visitors can experience the traditional art forms of Kerala. The town is also home to beautiful temples and is often referred to as the cultural capital of Kasargod.

maipadi palace

Maipady Palace is a historic royal residence located in the Kasargod district of Kerala, India. This ancient palace holds significant cultural and architectural value and is associated with the Kolathiri Rajas, the ruling dynasty of the region
