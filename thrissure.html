THRISSUR

Thrissur, often called the "Cultural Capital of Kerala", is one of the most vibrant and historically rich cities in the state. Located in the central part of Kerala, Thrissur is known for its festivals, cultural events, temples, and natural beauty

Vadakkumnathan Temple

This ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva is one of the most important landmarks in Thrissur. Known for its grand Kerala-style architecture, the temple is situated atop a hill and offers panoramic views of the surrounding city. The temple is a major center for religious and cultural activities in Thrissur

Thrissur Pooram

If you're visiting during the festival season, Thrissur Pooram is an absolute must-see. This grand temple festival is one of the largest and most famous in Kerala, known for its majestic elephant processions, fireworks, and traditional drum performances. It attracts thousands of tourists every year.

athirapally waterfalls

Athirappilly Waterfalls is located about 30 km from Thrissur and is the largest waterfall in Kerala. The waterfall, surrounded by lush green forests, offers a breathtaking view and is a popular spot for nature lovers and adventure seekers

Thrissur Zoo

The Thrissur Zoo (also known as the Sree Chitra Enclave Zoo) is one of the oldest zoos in India. It's home to various species of animals, birds, and reptiles
