MALAPPURAM

Malappuram is a picturesque district located in the northern part of Kerala, India. Known for its rich cultural heritage, scenic landscapes, and historical significance, Malappuram is a blend of natural beauty and vibrant traditions. The district is characterized by lush green hills, vast paddy fields, and the winding Nilgiri hills, making it a haven for nature lovers and photographers.

miniooty

Miniooty is a quaint village located in the Malappuram district of Kerala, India. Known for its natural beauty and tranquil surroundings, the village is a peaceful retreat that offers a glimpse into traditional rural life in Kerala. Surrounded by lush greenery, hills, and farmlands, Miniooty is ideal for those seeking serenity away from urban areas

kottakunnu park

Kottakunnu Park is a popular recreational park located in Malappuram, Kerala. Set on a hilltop, the park offers stunning panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including lush greenery and the town below. It is an ideal spot for family outings, picnics, and leisurely walks. The park is equipped with playgrounds, walking paths, and seating areas, making it perfect for relaxation and enjoying the outdoors.

nilambur teak musuem

Nilambur Teak Museum is located in Nilambur, a town in Malappuram district, Kerala, and is dedicated to showcasing the significance of teak wood in the region. The museum highlights the history, growth, and uses of teak, which is known as the "king of timber." The museum features exhibits on the different varieties of teak, its role in the local economy, and the history of teak plantations in Nilambur, which is home to one of the oldest teak plantations in the world

thirumandhamkunnu baghavadhi

Thirummaandhakunn Bhagavathy is a revered goddess worshiped in parts of Kerala, India, particularly in the region surrounding the town of Thirummaandhakunnu. This temple, dedicated to the goddess, holds immense religious significance for the local community.
